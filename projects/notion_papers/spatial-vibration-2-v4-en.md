Conversely, in vast cosmic voids, the intrinsic vibrational radiation pressure ($p_{vib} < 0$) emitted by the fluctuating spatial background is proposed as a repulsive effective field, providing a speculative mechanism for the accelerated expansion of the universe (dark energy). 

Furthermore, relativistic length contraction is heuristically reinterpreted as the 'Doppler Compression' of spatial waves, and micro-perturbations in gravitational lensing are suggested as potential observational markers. While remaining largely interpretive and requiring rigorous relativistic formulations, this paper outlines a conceptual pathway connecting microscopic spatial fluctuations with macroscopic cosmological phenomena.
